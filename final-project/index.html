A Painting Demonstration

(Reference Image)

  • Links to supplies used:
  • Paint set
  • Main paint brush (Size 8)
  • Secondary paint brush (Size 2)
  • Cardstock Paper
  • Paint palette

Step 1: First, start off by adding a light layer of water to the paper. This will help the paint glide across the paper more easily.

Then add a thin layer of paint colors that are similar to the reference image.

Step 2: For this step, add more layers of paint that are similar to the reference image. Make sure to allow the paint to dry between each layer to avoid the paper from shedding.

While adding the layers, move the paintbrush in a sweeping motion to blend out the colors at the bottom. For the water portion, add small wave-effects by gliding the paint brush unevenly across the paper.

Step 3: For the third step, I decided to add more details to the painting. To add more details to the water, use a smaller-sized brush to paint out thin stripes of different lengths across the body of the water with the bigger stripes being represented at the front of the water. Be sure to make these stripes darker than the body of the water.

In the background of the sky add some yellow rays around a white circle to represent the sun emitting light.

Step 4: In the last step, add some highlights and smaller details to the overall painting. To achieve this outlook, incorporate some thin lines on top of the waves. I made these lines similar to the color of the orange sky.

For the clouds, mix some red, blue, and a little bit of brown paint to make a dark purple cloud. Use the same smaller-sized brush to draw and blend out the clouds. For the final part, I have added my name to the bottom right corner of the painting.
